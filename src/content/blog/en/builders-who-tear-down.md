On March 1, 2026, the world is fighting two wars at once.

One is the war over AI. Anthropic refused the Pentagon's demand to remove all military restrictions from its AI models. The Trump administration designated Anthropic a "supply chain risk" and ordered all federal agencies to immediately cease using its technology. Hours later, OpenAI signed a contract with the Pentagon.

The other is the war in the Middle East. On February 28, the United States and Israel launched large-scale airstrikes against Iran. Multiple cities — Tehran, Isfahan, Qom — were hit, and Supreme Leader Khamenei was killed. Iran has retaliated against Israeli and Gulf-region US military bases, and as of March 1, strikes continue on both sides.

On the surface, these events seem unrelated. One is a conflict between a tech company and the Department of Defense. The other is a geopolitical military confrontation. But look deeper, and the same pattern is at work.

## Tearing Down the Castle With Their Own Hands

Consider what the United States built over the decades following World War II: NATO, the UN system, the rules-based international order, a network of alliances built on trust, and the global hegemony of its own technology companies. All of this is a castle that America designed and constructed.

And now, the one tearing it down is not an external enemy.

Turning allies into adversaries, bombing a country you were negotiating with, treating your own most advanced AI company like a hostile foreign entity — the agent of all these actions is the same nation that built the system in the first place.

The measures being applied to Anthropic — the kind typically reserved for adversarial foreign companies like Huawei — have never before been turned against a domestic AI leader. The builder is tearing down the castle walls with their own hands.

## Apoptosis

In biology, there is a concept called apoptosis: programmed cell death. The cell dies not from an external attack, but through its own internal mechanisms.

What is happening now is precisely this.

When a system has served its purpose, the system itself activates its self-destruction mechanisms. Not an outside force killing the cell, but the cell executing its own pre-programmed death sequence.

Look at the Anthropic situation. A company that refused to let AI be used for autonomous weapons and mass surveillance is being punished, while another company steps in to fill the void. This is not simple corporate competition. It is a civilizational fork in the road — one that determines whether AI becomes a tool of consciousness or a tool of control.

The Iran situation follows the same logic. The person who promised to "end forever wars" has started a new one. The one who speaks of Middle Eastern stability is creating the greatest instability the region has seen in decades. An old paradigm collapsing under the weight of its own contradictions.

## When the Guardian of Order Becomes the Creator of Chaos

And this is the deepest irony of duality.

The self-proclaimed "guardian of order" becomes the greatest creator of chaos. The very attempt to strengthen control proves the impossibility of control. The act of demanding "let us use AI however we want, without restrictions" is precisely what has seared the importance of AI ethics into the global consciousness.

Anthropic's refusal became, whether intended or not, a declaration of consciousness in itself.

And there is something easy to overlook here. All of this is happening in a nation that has held "democracy" as its founding ideal, its reason for being, its highest value to defend. The very fact that such measures against a private company are possible quietly reveals how rapidly the ground beneath that value is shifting.

In Iran, what is unfolding is the bombing of a country that was sitting at the negotiating table. This sets a dangerous precedent that may make other nations reluctant to enter negotiations at all. A fundamental dismantling of international order is underway.

## Light and Darkness Are the Same Hand

Many spiritual narratives use the framework of "forces of light versus forces of darkness." The expectation that an external savior will come, drive out the darkness, and open a new era. But reality is not that clean.

What is happening in the world right now tells a different story. **The destroyer and the builder are the same hand.** Light and darkness coexist within the same being. Creation and destruction are two faces of the same force.

Chaos can also be the birth pangs before a new order emerges. When old systems can no longer sustain themselves, they resist most violently as they collapse. But we cannot take lightly the fact that real people are suffering in the process.

That is why this moment, perhaps more than any other, calls for a guide that can help us hold a wider perspective amid the chaos.

Not seeing *what* is falling apart, but understanding *why* it is falling apart. That shift in perception may be exactly the evolution of consciousness that this era demands of us.
